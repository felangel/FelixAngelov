Logo, Business Card, Letterhead, & Envelope

March 7th, 2016

Since I wanted to focus on a web developer role for this project, I decided to use a color scheme similar to Sublime Text Editor (a program used to write and edit code). In addition, I wanted to create a logo that was simple yet conveyed a lot of information. The red letters are my initials and the format of the logo is supposed to represent HTML.

For the business card I, again, wanted to emphasize web development in a fun way. As a result, I decided to write my business card in javascript, another programming language used by web developers. The color scheme stayed true to the Sublime Text Editor and I decided to include my name and contact information as two separate functions. I also decided to add line numbers as you would normally see when writing code on the left hand side of the card and placed my logo in the upper right-hand corner. I chose not to design a back side in order for people to be able to write on the back or take notes.

For the letterhead, I wanted to keep the color scheme while not making the design too cluttered. I decided to take the contact information from my business card and keep it in the top right-hand corner with the logo on the top left. In order to keep the color scheme I added an abstract design in the top and layered the grey and blue. I then mirrored the top and layered grey with green so that the letter's content was enveloped in the same background colors giving the illusion of also being like code.

Lastly, for the envelope design I tried to keep it as simple as possible so I applied the same layering principle and pattern that I used in my letterhead, however, with three layers to maintain the similar color scheme. I reduced the contact information to only include the address alongside the logo similar to the letterhead and decided to avoid adding anything else in order to give the sender room to specify the address etc.
